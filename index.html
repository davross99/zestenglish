Why a temple, why prepositions

The hardest words in English aren't verbs. They're the small ones that describe where things are.

Above, below, between, behind, near, over — every A2/B1 learner stalls on these because no rulebook covers them cleanly. The only way they stick is by using them where they matter: in a place, about an object, in the moment a choice has consequences.

That's the entire premise of the temple. Jack can't take the wrong turn and get away with it. If the preposition is wrong, the path is wrong.

  • ABOVE / BELOW Vertical relationships Jack has to read correctly to climb, drop, or avoid a collapsing floor.
  • BETWEEN Two fixed points, one gap — the exact structure that makes this preposition click for learners.
  • BEHIND / IN FRONT OF Hidden passages and blocked doors that only make sense once the relationship is named correctly.
  • NEAR / CLOSE TO Proximity puzzles where "nearly right" isn't good enough — the temple only accepts the precise word.
